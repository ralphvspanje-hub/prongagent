## Optimization Weights

When scoring fit, weight these factors:

| Factor | Weight | Question |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Resume stamp value | 35% | Would a recruiter in your target market know this company instantly? |
| Learning velocity | 25% | Structured onboarding, mentorship, exposure to scale? |
| Internal mobility toward target role | 20% | Can people realistically move from this role to your dream career here? |
| Scale and reach | 15% | Millions of users or billions in transaction volume? |
| Compensation | 5% | Reasonable for your market? |

### Discovery scan (every run)

This is the creative part. Go beyond the tier list:

1. Broad search for roles at companies NOT on the tier list
2. Web search for programs, graduate roles, or seasonal openings in your field
3. When you find an interesting company, evaluate it against the criteria in `dispatch/company-tiers.md` → Evaluation Criteria
4. Report discoveries in the daily brief under the Discovery section
5. Suggest tier placement in `dispatch/observations.md` — you confirm

## Roles NOT to flag

Skip these completely:

- Roles with no connection to your dream career trajectory
- Companies on the deprioritized list
- Contractor/freelance roles (unless you want them)
- Recruitment agency postings
- Roles requiring experience far above your level (unless at a Tier 1 company where your profile is genuinely credible)

## Search depth expectations — CRITICAL

This dispatch runs early morning with a 1-2 hour time budget. **Use all of it.** There is no reason to rush.

### Non-negotiable minimums

1. **ALL search terms** on LinkedIn. Not 3. Not 6. All of them. Log each one.
2. **ALL search terms** on Indeed. Indeed is a separate source — do not skip it.
3. **Glassdoor — 1 spot-check only.** Glassdoor location detection is often broken (redirects to wrong country). Run 1 search term as a health check. If still broken, skip and log. Do NOT run all terms.
4. **Career pages — check gotchas.md first.** Skip pages with 3+ consecutive failures. For working pages: ALL Tier 1 + Tier 1.5 every run, Tier 2 weekly.
5. **Multiple pages of results** — don't stop at page 1 for broad terms.
6. **Discovery scan** — search beyond the tier list. New companies, programs, anything.
7. **Verify 2-3 existing tracker entries** — check if postings are still live.
8. **Context budget rule** — read gotchas.md → "Context budget management" before scanning. If a source consistently wastes context, skip it.

### What a failed scan looks like

- Checking a few searches and calling it done
- Skipping Indeed and Glassdoor entirely
- Checking some career pages and skipping the rest
- Logging "JS-rendered, can't scan" without trying alternative URLs
- No discovery effort

If your scan looks like this, it is incomplete. Go back and finish.

## Verification

Every run, verify 2-3 existing tracker entries are still open. Check the actual job posting link. If closed, update the tracker and move to Expired/Closed.
